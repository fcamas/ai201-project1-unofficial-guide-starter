LONG ISLAND
Seaside Escapes
iloveny.com/LI
This beach-lover’s paradise offers white-sand beaches, deep sea fishing, world-class wineries, farm-fresh
cuisine and fabulous shopping. Discover the Gold Coast mansions that inspired The Great Gatsby, championship
golf courses and historic lighthouses from Fire Island to Montauk. See a lunar module while exploring the
region’s legendary aviation history, tour whaling museums and take the kids to one of the nation’s best-rated
waterparks. Great places to stay range from seaside cottages to spa resorts.

NEW YORK CITY
Big Apple Thrills
iloveny.com/NYC
Climb to new heights with a visit to the city’s newest observation platform at SUMMIT One Vanderbilt, where
80-mile views include landmarks like the Empire State Building. Explore icons like the Statue of Liberty and
immerse yourself in the architectural wonders of The Oculus and scenic gardens at Little Island Park. See
world-class art at museums like the Museum of Modern Art (MoMA) and performances on Broadway and at
Lincoln Center. Great sports venues include Barclays Center and Citi Field. Make your way through all five
boroughs to experience eclectic neighborhoods that showcase a diversity of cultures and cuisines.

taking in an open-air performance. A variety of unique accommodations offer you the perfect retreat.

CAPITAL-SARATOGA
400 Years of History
iloveny.com/CS
Apple orchards and farmland surround this region’s historic cities. An eclectic mix of attractions includes
Albany’s magnificent Capitol building, the Rivers Casino Resort, interactive museums, beautiful state parks
and craft breweries and distilleries. Opulent theaters include The Palace in Albany and Proctor’s in Schenectady.
The 1875 Troy Savings Bank Music Hall attracts world-class musicians. Along with Saratoga Race Course, rated a
top 10 venue by Sports Illustrated, Saratoga Springs offers a performing arts center that’s the summer home of the
NYC Ballet. Nearby, a national historic park marks the site of the turning point of the Revolutionary War.

ADIRONDACKS
Wilderness Playground
iloveny.com/AD
Enjoy boundless activities amid six million acres of forever wild landscape. Stay in a rustic lakeside cabin
or grand resort. Hike the High Peaks, kayak in pristine lakes and bike between Adirondack Coast wineries.
Explore restaurants and shops in picturesque villages from Lake George to Lake Placid. Discover unique
museums, such as the Adirondack Experience or The Wild Center with its Wild Walk trail through the treetops.
Find one-of-a-kind thrills like summer bobsled runs at the Olympic Sports Complex. Visit Santa in his own
theme park in North Pole, NY.

1000 ISLANDS-SEAWAY
Picture-Perfect Coastlines
iloveny.com/1000
Along 200 miles of picture-perfect coastline, among more than 1,800 islands in one of America’s most beautiful
settings, discover historic Fort Ontario, splendid island castles, unique museums like the Antique Boat Museum,
and enchanting shoreline villages. Enjoy sandy freshwater beaches, scuba diving in the crystal-clear St. Lawrence,
scenic boat and hot-air balloon tours, and some of the world’s best trophy fishing. To relax, just sit back and
watch the great oceangoing ships and majestic sunsets on Great Lake Ontario.

UNIQUE LODGING
Whether you choose a waterside castle, cool mountain resort, wine country inn, canal boat, campsite, glampground,
farm stay or five-star hotel, New York’s one-of-a-kind accommodations add fun and adventure to your vacation.

Connecting Experiences, People and Places
EMPIRE STATE
TRAIL
Enjoy one of New York’s recreational wonders, the 750-mile Empire State Trail. A perfect route for
bicycling, hiking, running or walking, the trail consists of three main sections: the Hudson Valley
Greenway Trail, which extends from the Battery in Lower Manhattan up to Albany; the Champlain
Valley Trail, which heads north to Rouses Point; and the Erie Canalway Trail, which winds through
the Finger Lakes and ends in Buffalo. There is easy access all along the trail and designated parking
areas should you want to drive to a specific section (check the Empire State Trail map to find your
entryway and route). Best of all, The Empire State Trail will get you outside to enjoy nature with
countless diversions, from stopping for craft beverages and a bite to eat, to visiting world-class
museums and fascinating historic sites. For details, see empiretrail.ny.gov
